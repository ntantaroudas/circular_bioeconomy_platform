Introduction

The Circular Bioeconomy Smart Solution (SS) was developed to facilitate a transition to a circular bioeconomy in the Pongau region. This platform helps stakeholders to visualize resource flows, analyze sustainability options, and make informed decisions regarding natural resource management. The primary focus is on identifying and analyzing the potential valorization pathways of local residue streams such as biomass ashes, sewage sludge, and food waste, contributing to sustainable regional development.

Overview & Objectives

The Circular Bioeconomy SS designed by Alchemia Nova aims to facilitate the transition to a circular Bioeconomy by providing the stakeholders of Pongau with a comprehensive decision-making support system/matrix tool. Advanced techniques such as mind-mapping of resource flows, multi-criteria analysis, and material flows diagrams are carried out to provide views and visualizations of various circular Bioeconomy scenarios of interest for the area of Pongau. The simple-to-understand, circular Bioeconomy scenarios are disseminated in a way that is useful for stakeholders who would like to have detailed and understandable information on the sustainable management of natural resources. Through detailed analysis and feedback mechanisms the SS identifies and evaluates potential valorization pathways for different residue streams. In addition, it aims to facilitate the refinement of Bioeconomy scenarios based on stakeholder feedback and multi-criteria analysis. Furthermore, by analyzing three specific residue streams such as ashes, sewage sludge and food waste from hotels, valuable information on possible valorization pathways are provided, helping stakeholders to make informed decisions regarding resource utilization and illustrates how these alternatives can positively impact the region, contributing to its economic, environmental, and social sustainability. This approach aims to encourage the adoption of circular Bioeconomy practices, leading to reduced waste and enhanced resource efficiency in the area of Pongau.

Challenges from MAPs Interaction

During co-creation activities, several challenges were identified in the implementation of the Circular Bioeconomy SS. Language barriers, especially the preference for German, posed a significant obstacle, requiring multilingual support. Additionally, technical challenges related to waste conversion, transport, and financing were encountered. Another critical challenge stemmed from the fluctuating nature of waste production, particularly due to the region's reliance on seasonal tourism. Flexible and adaptive management strategies were required to handle these variations effectively. Stakeholder engagement also emerged as a key challenge but was addressed through regional governance partnerships.

Solution Concept and Scope

In the preliminary stages of co-creation activities with the Pongau MAP, an analysis of the current regional streams is carried out. This analysis involves specific inquiries into the origins of the materials, their processing methods, and their end-of-life scenarios. On the basis of this analysis, attempts are made to identify alternative valorization paths that can be used in conjuction with the available system. Additionally, technological alternatives are assessed through comparisons carried out based on different scenarios.

These scenarios are formulated using matrices and the comparison of mass flow diagrams is carried out to formulate coherent and easy to understand potential scenarios. Subsequently, alternative circular solutions and valorization pathways are developed. The focus is on the detailed analysis of individual residue streams, considering their emergence, their composition, their quantity, and other key factors. However, one key constraint of such analysis, is the data availability of these residue streams. As a result, engagement with regional politicians, decision-makers, and industry stakeholders is necessary in order to overcome this obstacle.

Technology Specifications

The development of the Circular Bioeconomy SS brings together a set of different tools to facilitate decision-making within the circular bioeconomy domain. In particular, the smart solution includes the following key components within its architecture concept:

  • Process Charts as Mind-Maps: These charts offer a visual representation of resource flows, allowing for intuitive understanding and analysis of the complex systems involved.
  • Multi-Criteria Analysis (MCA) Matrices: A structured approach to evaluate and compare the performance of different circular bioeconomy scenarios against a set of predefined criteria is provided through MCA Matrices.
  • Sankey Mass Flow Charts: Sankey mass flow charts are utilized to easily visualize the flow of materials through various processes. Sankey diagrams allow key users to visualize the efficiency and losses at each stage, offering a clear picture of material and energy balances.

The architecture of the Circular Bioeconomy SS is designed to integrate the key components of the circular bioeconomy SS shown in Figure into an inclusive modular platform. The system architecture is structured under a layered framework as follows:

- Data Input Layer: In the data Input layer, raw data on residue streams and process parameters are collected and entered into the system.
- Analysis Layer: In the analysis layer, mind-maps are used for initial visualization purposes, MCA matrices are used for scenario evaluation, and Sankey mass flow charts for flow analysis visualization and evaluation.
- Output Layer: Detailed reports, visualizations as well as useful insights from on the analysis layer are generated through the output layer.

The systematic approach presented previously helps users and stakeholders to navigate through complex data and derive meaningful insights to inform their decisions. One key feature of the Circular Bioeconomy SS is its suitability for application in different regions. In particular, the approach developed depends on circular economy and sustainability principles and can be implemented in any region according to its conditions and specific use cases under examination. This replicability is made possible through the flexible design of the mind-maps and MCA matrices, which can be customized to incorporate local variables and criteria, as well as the ability to model and visualize material flows specific to a regionâ€™s industries and resources using Sankey charts. This replicability, combined with specific tools and structured system architecture, makes the Circular Bioeconomy SS a versatile and impactful tool in advancing the goals of the circular bioeconomy, capable of addressing unique challenges and opportunities of diverse regions.

Specific requirements are derived and considered for the integration and compatibility of the Circular Bioeconomy SS with other systems or platforms in order to ensure smooth functionality and user experience. Integration requires the harmonization of data capture at the regional level (NUTS3) with existing databases at broader levels (NUTS2 & NUTS1) [1], ensuring that our solution can effectively communicate and utilize data from these sources. Additionally, exploring compatibility with platforms like vcg.ai presents a potential exploration field to enhance the SS's capabilities. On the compatibility side, the SS faces considerations with the production of Sankey (material flow diagrams), which can be generated either through the e!sankey software, a Windows-based application preferred for its ease of use, or via Python code, which necessitates different Excel layouts. This dual-path approach necessitates careful planning to accommodate users' varying technical preferences and system requirements.

Regarding deployment, support, and maintenance, plans are underway to possibly have the Pongau MAP host a building material exchange platform, which could significantly enhance regional circular economy efforts. In addition, to improve open governance in Pongau district, it is being examined the possibility of setting up a dashboard as a tool on the Pongau website ( www.pongau.org ). This would provide an easy way to access information, also considering data from the Pongau MAP. This deployment strategy, coupled with considerations for long-term service availability and maintenance, will provide a sustainable and impactful solution that addresses the needs of the Pongau region even beyond the end of the project. __

Vacant Buildings

The focus on vacant buildings emerged from the region's need for improved vacancy management tools. Instead of letting these spaces remain unused, the initiative aims to identify and categorize vacant buildings based on their potential for new uses, such as tourism or bioeconomy. This involves leveraging existing regional data to facilitate the repurposing of these buildings effectively. A capacity-building workshop with Pongau stakeholders explored barriers, drivers, needs, and best practices for vacancy management. Given the high regional priority for reutilizing and maintaining vacant buildings, efforts will be made to integrate this need into the Circular Bioeconomy Smart Solution. The categorization of vacant buildings for circular bioeconomy purposes will build upon existing research from ANRI and collaboration with the Pongau region. The solution will be implemented within the Bioeconomy online platform, offering an interactive database that helps identify and activate suitable vacant buildings for circular bioeconomy applications. Additionally, it will enable communication with property owners through the regional channel of the Regionalverband Pongau, ensuring a streamlined approach to repurposing these spaces.
