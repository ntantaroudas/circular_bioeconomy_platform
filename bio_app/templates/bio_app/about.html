Overview & Objectives

The Circular Bioeconomy SS designed by Alchemia aims to facilitate the transition to a circular bioeconomy by providing the stakeholders of the MAP in Pongau with a comprehensive decision-making support system/matrix tool. Advanced techniques such as mind-mapping of resource flows, multi-criteria analysis, and material flow diagrams will be carried out to provide views and visualizations of various circular bioeconomy scenarios that are of interest to the MAP. The simple-to-understand, circular bioeconomy scenarios will be disseminated in a way that is useful for stakeholders who would like to have detailed and understandable information on the sustainable management of natural resources. Through detailed analysis and feedback mechanisms the SS identifies and evaluates potential valorization pathways for different residue streams. In addition, it aims to facilitate the refinement of bioeconomy scenarios based on stakeholder feedback and multi-criteria analysis.

The SS will promote sustainable regional development by highlighting the benefits of alternative valorization pathways. Furthermore, by analyzing three specific residue streams such as ashes, sewage sludge and food waste from hotels, valuable information on possible valorization pathways will be provided, helping stakeholders to make informed decisions regarding resource utilization and will Illustrate how these alternatives can positively impact the region, contributing to its economic, environmental, and social sustainability. This approach aims to encourage the adoption of circular bioeconomy practices, leading to reduced waste and enhanced resource efficiency in the area of Pongau.

Challenges from MAPs interaction

Specific co-creation activities have been carried out between the SS provider (Alchemia) and Pongau region MAP. During these activities it was identified that a significant challenge is the language barrier, with a preference for the German language, highlighting the need for multilingual support. In addition, several technical challenges have been identified regarding the selected residue streams. These challenges include establishing conversion steps, addressing transport and logistics, sizing, costs, and financing. Furthermore, in regions such as Pongau, engaging stakeholders effectively presents an additional challenge. This issue however, is being effectively addressed through regional governance partnerships.

The emergence of residue fluctuates significantly due to seasonal tourism and as a result flexible and adaptive management strategies are required to accommodate these variations. During the development of the SS, several challenges have been encountered, including difficulties in estimating the technical feasibility of alternative valorization pathways. This was largely due to missing information regarding TRLs during literature researching in the existing state of the art.

Additionally, the main focus has predominantly been on developing a circular bioeconomy pathway for the Pongau region, leaving less time to tackle challenges related to the online tool itself and the development and integration of a material exchange platform to facilitate the SS. To address these issues, solutions such as consulting regional experts and key stakeholders twice to validate proposed pathways, and considering the use of existing platforms or flagship projects for circularization of building materials have been examined.

Despite these efforts, ongoing and anticipated challenges persist, including translating the methodology to an online tool, engaging all stakeholders effectively, and implementing the innovations. Interactions with MAPs have further highlighted the challenge of aligning diverse stakeholder perspectives and integrating their feedback, necessitating a continuous dialogue, iterative design updates, and a flexible framework to accommodate evolving requirements and insights.

Solution Concept and Scope

In the preliminary stages of co-creation activities with the Pongau Map, an analysis of the current regional residue streams is carried out. This analysis involves specific inquiries into the origins of the materials, their processing methods, and their end-of-life scenarios. On the basis of this analysis, attempts are made to identify alternative valorization paths that can be used in conjunction with the available system. Additionally, technological alternatives are assessed through comparisons carried out based on different scenarios.

These scenarios are formulated using matrices and the comparison of mass flow diagrams is carried out to formulate coherent and easy to understand potential scenarios. Subsequently, alternative circular solutions and valorization pathways are developed. The focus is on the detailed analysis of individual residue streams, considering their emergence, their composition, their quantity, and other key factors. However, one key constraint of such analysis, is the data availability of these residue streams. As a result, engagement with regional politicians, decision-makers, and industry stakeholders is necessary in order to overcome this obstacle.

Technology Specifications

The development of the Circular Bioeconomy SS brings together a set of complex sophisticated tools to facilitate decision-making within the circular bioeconomy domain. In particular the smart solution includes the following key components within its architecture concept:

Process Charts as Mind-Maps: These charts offer a visual representation of resource flows, allowing for intuitive understanding and analysis of the complex systems involved.
Multi-Criteria Analysis (MCA) Matrices: A structured approach to evaluate and compare the performance of different circular bioeconomy scenarios against a set of predefined criteria is provided through MCA Matrices.
Sankey Mass Flow Charts: Sankey mass flow charts are utilized to easily visualize the flow of materials through various processes. Sankey diagrams allow key users to visualize the efficiency and losses at each stage, offering a clear picture of material and energy balances.

The architecture of the Circular Bioeconomy SS is designed to integrate the key components of the circular bioeconomy SS shown in Figure into an inclusive modular platform. The system architecture is structured under a layered framework as follows:

Data Input Layer: In the data Input layer, raw data on residue streams and process parameters are collected and entered into the system.
Analysis Layer: In the analysis layer, mind-maps are used for initial visualization purposes, MCA matrices are used for scenario evaluation, and Sankey mass flow charts for flow analysis visualization and evaluation.
Output Layer: Detailed reports, visualizations as well as useful insights from on the analysis layer are generated through the output layer.

The systematic approach presented previously helps users and stakeholders to navigate through complex data and derive meaningful insights to inform their decisions. One key feature of the Circular Bioeconomy SS is its suitability for application in different regions. In particular, the approach developed depends on circular economy and sustainability principles and can be implemented in any region according to its conditions and specific use cases under examination. This replicability is made possible through the flexible design of the mind-maps and MCA matrices, which can be customized to incorporate local variables and criteria, as well as the ability to model and visualize material flows specific to a regionâ€™s industries and resources using Sankey charts. This replicability, combined with the specific tools and structured system architecture, makes the Circular Bioeconomy SS a versatile and impactful tool in advancing the goals of the circular bioeconomy, capable of addressing unique challenges and opportunities of diverse regions.

Specific requirements are derived and considered for the integration and compatibility of the Circular Bioeconomy SS with other systems or platforms in order to ensure smooth functionality and user experience. Integration requires the harmonization of data capture at the regional level (NUTS3) with existing databases at broader levels (NUTS2 & NUTS1) [1], ensuring that our solution can effectively communicate and utilize data from these sources. Additionally, exploring compatibility with platforms like vcg.ai presents a potential exploration field to enhance the SS's capabilities. On the compatibility side, the SS faces considerations with the production of Sankey (material flow diagrams), which can be generated either through the e!sankey software [2], a Windows-based application preferred for its ease of use, or via Python code, which necessitates different Excel layouts. This dual-path approach necessitates careful planning to accommodate users' varying technical preferences and system requirements.

Regarding deployment, support, and maintenance, plans are underway to possibly have the Pongau MAP host a building material exchange platform, which could significantly enhance regional circular economy efforts. In addition, to improve open governance in Pongau district, it is being examined the possibility of setting up a dashboard as a tool on the Pongau website ( www.pongau.org ). This would provide an easy way to access information, also considering data from the Pongau MAP. This deployment strategy, coupled with considerations for long-term service availability and maintenance, will provide a sustainable and impactful solution that addresses the needs of the Pongau region even beyond the end of the project. __
